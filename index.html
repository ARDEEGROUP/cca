Ardee Foundation, known for its contribution to education and philanthropy for over four decades, presents CCA (Centre for Culture and Art), a space for culture, thought and art. An eclectic space that nurtures diverse disciplines including visual and performing art, literature, film and theatre.

CCA was born under the guiding light of the visionary Mr. Ashok Varma in the late 80s. Ahead of its time in many ways, CCA rode the wave of an emerging art eco-system, showcasing the art of stalwarts like Manjit Bawa, Raza, M.F. Hussein, Krishen Khanna, Ram Kumar, Souza, Swaminathan, Arpita Singh and many others
Forty years later the founder’s grandson, young art graduate Imaan Varma, is quietly creating a first-of-its-kind creative space in the heart of Delhi. CCA is envisioned as a collectors home, a space where guests engage with the ever-evolving art on the walls of the living rooms, research in its vast library and where the large sculpture gardens and amphitheatre becomes a spot for creative reflection, thought and dialogue.

A talent incubator since the 90s, CCA will also continue its patronage in its studios, supporting young and emerging artists who promise to be the future masters of India.

A vibrant cultural annual programme with senior artists, curators, art historians, museum directors will offer an inspirational range of experiences and interactions which keep bringing guests back for more.
